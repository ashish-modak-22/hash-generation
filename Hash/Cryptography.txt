Cybersecurity refers to the practice of protecting computers, networks, and data from unauthorized access, attacks, and damage. Its history dates back to the early days of computing in the 1970s, when the first computer viruses and worms began to appear. As the internet expanded in the 1990s, cyber threats also increased, leading to the development of firewalls, antivirus software, and encryption techniques. Over time, cybersecurity has evolved into a critical field with advanced tools like intrusion detection systems, AI-based threat analysis, and zero-trust security models.

The advantages of cybersecurity are numerous. It protects sensitive information such as personal data, financial records, and government secrets from cybercriminals. It also ensures the smooth functioning of businesses by preventing data breaches and system downtime. Strong cybersecurity builds trust among users and supports safe digital communication.

In today’s digital world, cybersecurity is a necessity rather than a choice. With the rise of online banking, social media, cloud computing, and smart devices, the amount of data being generated is massive. This makes systems more vulnerable to attacks like hacking, phishing, and ransomware. Therefore, cybersecurity is essential to safeguard digital life, maintain privacy, and ensure national and economic security.
